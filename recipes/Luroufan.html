Lu Rou Fan (Taiwanese Braised Pork Belly)

This is my take on Lu Rou Fan, a Taiwanese braised pork belly dish. It takes a bit of time to make the broth and to braise, but it is so worth it! It's best to cook in a large portion since it takes a long time to cook!

Servings:10-12

Ingredients

Pork

  • 6lb pork belly
  • 2Tbs minced garlic
  • 1Tbs ginger

Sauce Group 1

  • 3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1.5tsp five spice
  • 1/2 cup rock sugar
  • 1/2 cup light soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup dark soy sauce
  • 1 cup xiao xing wine

Sauce Group 2

  • 1/2 cup shallot sauce
  • 1/2 cup fried shallots

Broth

  • 1Tbs dried shrimp
  • 10 dried mushrooms
  • 1 piece kalp
  • Bonito soup base (optional)

Steps

  1. Submerge the pork belly in cold water overnight. I put them in the fridge submerged in the water. (alternatively, you can par boil the meat) in order to get rid of the porky smell
  2. In a medium pot, put all the broth ingredients and boil them with 8 cups of water
  3. Cut pork into 1x1in cube size
  4. In a large dutch oven or a pot, fry the pork cubes for at least 10 mins. (I tried to fry them as long as possible - until water evaporated or until they got brown) towards the end, add minced garlic and ginger to stir fry for another 5mins.
  5. Add all the 'Sauce group 1.' Add broth until pork is barely submerged in the pot (you may not use up all 8 cups of broth. I always seem to have left over borth, which I can use for other soup)
  6. Bring them to boil. Once it boils, reduce the heat to medium low and let it lightly boil for 45min-1hr, lid closed.
  7. In a separate pot, boil 12 eggs to the doneness of your liking.
  8. Add the 'Sauce group 2' and boiled eggs and bring it to hard boil once more.
  9. Once it boils, reduce the heat to medium low and let it lightly boil until the liquid gets reduced and thickens (usually another 1-2hrs), lid open.
